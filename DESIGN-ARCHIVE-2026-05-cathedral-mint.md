## Original direction (preserved verbatim from `.planning/PROJECT.md`)

**quartermint.com — Cathedral Hybrid Narrative**

A personal founder site live at quartermint.com that tells the story of a builder-operator with a thesis and a body of work. Features an embedded AI chat interface (streaming via Claude Sonnet 4.6), empathy-first system showcases, an investor-facing /invest route, per-system detail pages, and a visual design system rooted in editorial typography and mint color palette. The site demonstrates the thesis: information routed to the right person, in the right form, at the right time.

**Core Value:** A visitor with no context understands what Ryan builds and why within 60 seconds — and can interact with the site to get answers in real time via the embedded chat.

## Original visual system (preserved)

- **Display:** Instrument Serif
- **Body:** DM Sans
- **Accent:** `#A8E6CF` (mint)
- **Surface:** `#E6F4F1` (mint-tinted)
- **Background:** `#FFFFFF` light / `#1A1D23` dark (prefers-color-scheme only, no toggle)
- **Text:** `#333A45` / `#E8ECF0`
- **Spacing:** 80px section vertical (desktop)

The mint palette was intentional — it signaled a personal site, not a generic SaaS, and the editorial serif/sans pairing was the right move for a narrative-driven founder portfolio.

## Why it was retired

The Editorial Treasury direction (Fraunces + Geist + Ledger Green + Parchment + entity-geometry mark system) is calibrated for:

1. YC partners reviewing the S26 application
2. Amalgamated Bank distribution conversations
3. Political ops directors / treasurers
4. Compliance counsel and auditors

The mint palette and Instrument Serif read as personal/wellness/startup. For a private-fintech-pitched-to-institutional-partners product, Editorial Treasury wins on differentiation and audience fit. See `DESIGN.md` for the canonical system.
