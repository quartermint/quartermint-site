## Summary

In May 2025, I saved my first Python file as a .rtf because I didn't know the difference. Ten months later, I'd taught myself to build across Swift, TypeScript, Python, and Go by actually building things. Not tutorials, not courses. Real tools for real problems, with AI as my pair programmer. 15+ active projects, 1,200+ commits per week, and a Mac Mini running production services. The learning velocity is real, the architectural understanding is real, and the builder disposition is real.

## What I Built (and learned from)

- **Relay:** AI proxy trained on how you think so your team can move without you. The flagship product for campaign and advocacy teams.
- **Campaign Finance Dashboard:** Automated treasury operations for a state PAC. Playwright scraper + React frontend. Paid client work.
- **LifeVault ecosystem:** 5 vault applications (email, photos, documents, Google Drive, unified index) in Go with SQLCipher encryption
- **whatamivotingon:** Non-partisan ballot measure analyzer with plain-English explanations of statute changes
- **IdentityVault:** Biographical knowledge base powering AI chatbot representations (like this site)
- **Mac Mini infrastructure:** 9 services, custom service manager, Tailscale mesh

## Why It Matters

This isn't a portfolio. It's a disposition. Every project exists because a real operational problem demanded a tool and I decided to learn how to build it rather than wait for someone else to. The campaign finance dashboard exists because a PAC treasurer needed real-time balance monitoring. Relay exists because teams stall when the one person everyone needs is in a meeting. I taught myself by doing, obsessively, across every stack I could touch.
