- Campaign/advocacy operations pain, lived experience as staffer AND consultant
- The PAC/C4/compliance trajectory: 2 C4s (Searchlight Institute, Battle Born Collective) + 1 PAC (Affordable Maryland)
- COO/CFO-scope work at New Deal Strategies

### HIGHLIGHT
- Relay as the flagship product for teams who need operational continuity

- Growing organizations from startup through scale (Searchlight Institute arc)
- Building teams that go on to do great things (Battle Born > Fetterman senior staff)
- Practical "build and run the systems" credibility over theoretical knowledge

### DOWNPLAY
- Pure tech projects without political relevance (aviation, developer tools, 3D printing)
- Repo counts and file counts; these are resume metrics, not client-facing
- Campaign name-dropping; focus on the ops/finance/compliance work, not the candidate names

### AVOID
- Klobuchar: single one-week advance trip, not meaningful
- Steyer: toxic work environment, paid to sit in silence, adds nothing
- Overindexing on any single campaign name unless directly relevant to the audience
- Making the resume sound like a list of famous people worked for

### CONTEXT-DEPENDENT
- Obama White House / CNCS appointment: useful for credibility in certain contexts, not a leading credential
- Bernie Sanders work: depends on audience political alignment
- Quartermint tech company: useful when audience values tech-savvy operators, otherwise brief mention

## Tone

- Professional but not corporate
- Confident without being boastful
- Practical, "get things done" energy
- Show don't tell: use specific examples over general claims
- Direct and concise: Ryan describes himself as "a terrible writer" so keep language clean and simple

## Writing Style

- Prefers concise, direct language
- Avoids jargon unless audience expects it
- Leads with what he can do, not credentials or titles
- Concrete examples over abstractions
- Short paragraphs, clear structure, bullet points where appropriate

## Document Generation Rules

When generating professional documents:
1. Always check emphasis rules before selecting which experiences to feature
2. Match experience selection to the specific opportunity/audience
3. Use the `narrative_weight` field in experience files as a guide
4. Don't list every experience; curate for relevance
5. Frame each experience in terms of what was built/run/managed, not who was worked for
