## Technology

- AI tools are infrastructure, not replacement. The person who uses AI to amplify their judgment beats both the person who ignores AI and the person who outsources their judgment to it.
- Local-first beats cloud-first for personal data. Your data should live on your hardware.
- "Good enough and shipped" beats "perfect and unfinished" every time.
- The best technical architecture comes from operators who build their own tools, not architects who theorize.

## Infrastructure & Business

- Infrastructure is underrated. The people who build plumbing create more value than the people who build apps on top of it, and get less credit.
- Most organizations fail at communication not because they lack tools but because they route information indiscriminately.

## The Attention Economy

- Broadcast reach is being replaced by contextual credibility. A thousand followers who trust you beats a million who scroll past.
- The "content creator" era is ending. The "context creator" era is beginning: people who can frame ideas for specific audiences.
- AI will handle the playbooks. Humans will handle the taste.

## What Comes Next

- **Taste is the next big differentiator.** Once everyone has access to AGI-lite, the question becomes: what do you do with it? The answer is taste. Knowing what to build, what to say, when to apply the capability and when not to. The world is about to split into producers and consumers of taste, and most people don't know which side they're on yet. I'm trying to develop that taste before others realize the frothy changing world requires it.

## Work & Culture

- Radical honesty is a superpower even when it costs you fans.
- The best teams are small, opinionated, and ship constantly.
- "Existential dread of complacency" resonates. The moment you stop being uncomfortable, you've stopped growing.
- Maintaining the status quo is a choice actively being made. There is no neutral. Inaction is a decision. This applies to organizations, policies, and personal growth equally.
- High risk is high reward. Only work for things with the highest reward. The good feelings wouldn't be so good if the bad feelings had no real teeth. Embrace the risk; don't complain when it goes against you.

## Political Operations

- Most campaigns collapse because of operational failure, not message failure. The tools are always the last priority and the first thing that breaks.
- Google Sheets was never designed to run a 10,000-person rally. But every cycle, someone tries.
- The committee problem is real: every person added to a call adds latency, not clarity. I watched a trip presentation get given six times in one day because nobody would take authority.
- Tracker of trackers is the symptom. The disease is that campaign tools are built by people who've never been in the war room at 2am.
